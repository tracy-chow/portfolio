App Shopping on the Curricular Map

Empowering users with accessible and meaningful information to make just-in-time decisions in the classroom @ Avenues World School with SQL and Tableau

Overview

‍

Avenues is an independent nursery-12th grade dual language immersion school network headquartered in NYC with campuses in East Hampton, Sāo Paulo, Brazil, Shenzhen, China, and Silicon Valley. In addition, Avenues @Home enabled families to bring private teachers into their homes while Avenues Online served over 200 students from 25+ countries during my tenure. After chatting with educators about technology integration at the school, one of the biggest barriers to them integrating technology was the tedious and complicated process they had to go through to select and deploy apps for their students. I brought this to the attention of the director of technology and asked if I could redesign the workflow because teachers should have the tools they need to teach without having to spend days trying to procure them. My role for this particular project was to optimize the process of app selection by teachers and to come up with a workflow that would follow a functionally minimalist design.

‍

‍

1. Problem

Teachers needed a way to push out apps quickly for just-in-time and differentiated learning without needing to wait days (and sometimes up to two weeks) before an app could be pushed out to their students.

The IT team annually updated, organized & re-organized the overwhelming amounts of information about apps they had collected for almost 10 years. Unfortunately, it was done without consultation or usability testing with the actual end users. Moreover, no members of the IT team had taught as a full-time classroom teacher and so they were unfamiliar with the common pain points teachers faced.

Teachers indicated that the previous solutions did not synthesize the information to give them any useful insights. Instead, teachers went directly to the IT department for assistance.

‍

2. User Research Summary

The main pain points were related to:

• Timeliness:  teachers reported being overwhelmed by the existing "list" of apps and needing to wait for a consultation with the technology integration team for every app they wanted to test out, purchase, or use with their students.

• Usefulness of Info: Difficult to find apps that aligned with various learning objectives. In addition, they wanted to connect with colleagues who had experience using the app for tips and tricks.

• Ease of Use: Searches had to be done serially by categories. Teachers wanted to find apps that fulfilled a list of requirements.

‍

3. Solution & Results

A Tableau interactive data visualization connects the IT teams resources to quickly meet the needs of teachers and students and enable teachers to deploy the apps as they deem necessary to students from the school's MDM (Jamf Pro's Self-Service).

‍

Technical & Pedagogical Requirements

During app selection and approval, several criteria must be fulfilled:

• Alignment with curriculum & learning objectives

• Meets the school's privacy requirements for student data (e.g., COPPA, FERPA, etc)

• Available in English AND in the school's other target languages of instruction. For the initial rollout, Spanish & Mandarin with simplified Chinese characters were required.

• Available on multiple platforms and can be used on school devices (i.e., Apple devices)

• Approved for purchase based on subscription costs, budget lines, etc

‍

‍

From a pedagogical perspective, the existing solution of meeting with a technology integrator to gain access to test an app:

• Isolates app usage and lesson ideas to individual classrooms instead of facilitating collaboration between educators

• IT team becomes the gatekeeper of innovative uses for technological tools

At most schools, teachers must check in with the IT team, even if an app has already been approved for another class or grade level. This requires setting up a consultation with a technology integrator, explaining why they need to use the app, determining how to deploy the app, and then waiting again for the IT team to push out the apps from the school's MDM.

Understanding the User

1. Research Methodology

Field research and interviews with key stakeholders & end users were conducted to determine the pain points & opportunities for a platform where information about the school's curriculum & technology resources can be accessed for curricular planning & teaching.

‍

2. Research Questions

1. What are the goals of an educator looking for an app? What are the elements needed for a MVP?

2. What type of criteria or requirements are teachers most interested in during app selection?

‍

3. Participants

Four teachers who use apps with their students for class activities were interviewed to determine the pain points they experienced to inform the design of the product in question. Teachers taught at various levels from Pre-K to 5th grade in both English and Mandarin Chinese immersion classrooms. Two of the teachers had 5+ years of head teaching experience.

This user group confirmed the initial assumption that searching and deploying an app for just-in-time learning (e.g., differentiating math work) was extremely difficult due to the lengthy vetting process and the lead-time needed from request to use the app to final deployment to student devices. It was also one of the factors that prevented teachers from trying apps out with their students to enhance classroom activities. Users reported frustration with the need to consult with a technology integrator even when an app previously used and approved required the same lengthy process for deployment.

Value Proposition

• Self-serving, interactive & asynchronous app testing for teachers.

• Encourage teachers to integrate technology with lower time investment and the need to "get it right" on their first try when deploying an app to enhance or extend a learning activity.

• Instant access to recommended solutions based on learning objectives, language of instruction, technical requirements, curricular areas, and age levels right from the teacher's device.

• Empowers teachers to try out apps in their classrooms in a low-risk and high-reward environment.

• Teachers and administrators from global campuses can now collaborate and draw on the expertise of others as they integrate technology into their teaching & learning.

• Enables timely and easily accessible tools for teachers to use as they need it for differentiated and just-in-time learning.

• Reduces IT teams workload on redundant work and enables the team to work on other innovative solutions for teachers & students.

• Increases ROI for IT purchases by ensuring usage by teachers & students are maximized.

Outcomes & Impact

• 400+ views of the Apps Curriculum Scope & Sequence interface within the first month of initial rollout to technology & library educators in the United States, China, and Brazil. The actual tool made in Tableau can be tested out below.

‍

‍

• The director of libraries and the head librarian in Sāo Paulo were inspired to create an interactive data visualization for younger students to enable them to find books in a more simplified way. In addition, they expressed excitement about being able to gradually add features to the UI as students developed their research and reading skills.

• The tool was designed in a way so that it could be updated easily by other colleagues on the IT team.

‍

Takeaways & Next Steps

• Accessibility of these visualizations requires further action to comply with WCAG guidelines. For example, alt text for screen readers should be included. In addition, the mouse-heavy interface becomes difficult to use for anyone who mainly uses the keyboard.

• Scaling this to other campuses is possible with the creation of a standardized workflow.

• Connect with the infrastructure technology team to enable teachers to deploy a pre-approved list of apps from their Apple School Manager.

• Branding such as colors and fonts need to be updated to reflect the recent changes in the school's branding guidelines.

• Accessibility to information, ease of deployment, and ability to connect or see the projects of other educators using the app are crucial to lowering the barriers of entry for teachers who may be hesitant to use technology in the classroom.

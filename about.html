My Story

I am a frequent traveler (39 countries on 6 continents and counting). Twice an immigrant, I have also lived and studied in 3 different countries. I thrive in ambiguous situations and solving novel problems as I prefer a variety of ideas, stories, and challenges in my work and travels. I possess a growth mindset and enjoy learning from others. I thrive on finding patterns in chaos and organizing them into meaningful information that we can act on. To problem solve well requires empathy, and empathy requires embracing the diversity of perspectives balanced with the ability to be strategic & resourceful.

My unconventional career journey has equipped me with a skillset that's quite unique but much needed as a UX researcher, product developer, and user advocate. With my training in cognitive science, specifically in natural language processing,, visual processing, and the interactions between the two, I have a strong foundation in understanding how humans think and process information. In addition, I have collaborated with world-renowned researchers on experimental quantitative & qualitative studies in cognitive science funded by the United State's National Institutes of Health, National Science Foundation, Department of Homeland Security, and the Natural Science & Engineering Research Council of Canada.

In my work as an educator and as a technology integrator, I've had a chance to teach design thinking to elementary school children, partnering with organizations like NYU Tandon School of Engineering, to empower the next generation with the design & problem thinking tools to make an impact for their community.

Tapping into the intersection of my many interests and embracing my identity as a multipotentialite, I am ready to make an impact on a larger scale! After taking the initiative to own multiple projects and programs from ideation to delivery, I am energized and excited to create simple, useful, and well-loved solutions to enable others to make a positive impact, too.

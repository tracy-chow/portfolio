A Simplified Curriculum Mapping Alternative

Designing & rapid-prototyping a simplified and integrated curriculum mapping alternative for teachers by teachers @ Brooklyn Friends School with Google Apps Script & Looker

Project Management Phase

1. Project Duration

February 2021 - March 2021

‍

2. Credits & Background Note

This project was initiated by the Assistant Head of Lower School Academics at Brooklyn Friends School (client). Upon hearing about Richard Anderson's work on creating a documentation workflow for teachers using Google Apps Script with data from a Google Form, we embarked on creating something similar to solve the pain points many teachers (myself included) had in regards to documenting their work with the school's curriculum mapping app (Atlas Rubicon). Additional features were also added to meet the unique needs of the Lower School.

‍

3. Creating a Process & Setting Standards

Communication Frequencies: weekly meetings were agreed upon as times for check-ins to determine whether changes to features were needed and to test completed features.

Documentation Strategies: client requested documentation within the source code to help her follow along in the development process and for her to propose changes as needed.

Definition of Done-ness: client to test features in weekly meetings to indicate whether their needs have been met.

‍

4. Managing Risks

As this project was in the very early phases of its development, the client wanted to see and test a proof-of-concept before presenting the prototype to the senior leadership team. Documentation in the source code was requested to help the administrator follow along with the development of the tool.

Settings for a Shared Google Drive would have been determined after presentation of the working prototype to senior leadership and after gathering input from teachers. Historically, teachers at each grade level shared their lesson plans with each other but not across grade levels. Their buy-in would be needed should the project move forward. In addition, viewing vs. editing permissions would need to be further defined for users of a Shared Drive.

‍

5. Performing Estimations

Presentation of the high-fidelity prototype was not expected until at least July 2021. However, due to my departure from the school, we were unable to move forward with that phase of the project since the school was unable to find a colleague who could take over the project.

‍

Initial Task Development Estimations (after taking into other scheduling considerations):

  1. Discovery & Research = 1 week

  5. Google Apps Script to Save Lesson Plan in the Correct Folder = 1 week

Specification Phase

1. Problem

Curriculum documentation was uneven across the multiple divisions at Brooklyn Friends School. Depending on the divisional head, some teachers had to document their lesson plans in Atlas Rubicon. Some division heads were satisfied with documentation in spreadsheets and Shared Drives as alternatives. All teachers, however, agreed that they disliked documenting their work in Atlas - even if they understood the value of using a curriculum app. In the Lower School, documentation on Atlas required so much extra time and effort that it was done only once per school year on a day designated by the administrative team.

‍

2. Identifying Needs & Wants

The information added to the Atlas system was overwhelming for some users and it was difficult for them to access info to quickly answer a question (e.g., cross-grade collaborations). With the Lower School Assistant Head (of Academic Curriculum), we prioritized and added her "must-have" features and addressed problems commonly voiced by teachers:

â€¢ Atlas, the existing curriculum mapping app, was expensive and administrators faced a lot of unhappy teachers for a large sum ($10,000+). Could we create an alternative that would cost less than the existing yearly expense for Atlas?

â€¢ Teachers needed a way to quickly add & revise documentation using a system integrated with Google Drive (their tool of choice).

â€¢ Teachers and administrators needed a way to see what lessons across subject areas & grade levels were being taught from a bird's eye perspective.

â€¢  From these "bird's eye" views, they wanted a way to view the details of the lessons as well.

â€¢ Administrators wanted to a consistent template used for lesson planning.

â€¢ Neither teachers nor administrators wanted to complete unnecessary work.
